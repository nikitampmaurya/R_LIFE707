Reporting results

Remember to put a numbered figure legend below each figure. e.g. Figure 1. Stem height (in mm) for edible and poisonous mushrooms

For a t-test, your job is to convert R output to readable text. Something like: The average stem height for edible mushrooms was 7.07mm ( 3.76mm s.d., n = 2268) and for poisonous mushrooms was 6.18mm ( 3.07mm s.d., n= 2732), which was found to be significantly different using a Welch two sample t-test (t=9.099, d.f. = 4361.7, p < 0.001). This is shown in Figure 1..

You could also write Edible mushrooms had a significantly larger stem height (7.073.76mm s.d., n = 2268) than poisonous mushrooms (6.183.07mm s.d., n= 2732; two sample Welch t-test, t=9.099, d.f. = 4361.7, p < 0.001).

The important things are that the reader should know which group is bigger, what the sizes are, what test is used and the test statistic and p-value.

For a Chi-square test, something like, Colour was not significantly associated with whether a mushroom was edible or poisonous (Chi-square = 5.61, d.f. = 2, n.s.). But if there were a difference, youd have to explain which cell of the table had more individuals than expected (e.g.if youd found that red mushrooms tended to be poisonous).
